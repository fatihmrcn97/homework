First News Coming from Ukrain

Ukraine's entrant for the Eurovision Song Contest, Maruv, has been dropped in a row related to tensions between her country and Russia.

Maruv's song, Siren Song, won a public vote on Saturday, but the singer later fell out with the country's national broadcaster. She was given 24 hours to sign a contract that temporarily barred her from playing in Russia. Talks stalled on Monday and the broadcaster revoked Maruv's nomination. The 27-year-old, whose name real name is Anna Korsun, said she was prepared to cancel her next tour of Russia, but that other stipulations in the contract amounted to "censorship".
